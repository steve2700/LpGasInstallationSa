## Gas Stove Installation Process (400 words)

Professional gas stove installation requires precision and safety compliance. Here's our process:

### Step 1: Pre-Installation Assessment
We visit your property to:
- Measure your space and cutout requirements
- Check existing gas supply or plan new installation
- Assess ventilation requirements
- Verify electrical requirements (if applicable)
- Recommend suitable stove options
- Provide detailed quote

### Step 2: Preparation
Before installation day:
- Remove old electric stove (if applicable)
- Prepare gas supply line to cooktop location
- Cut countertop opening (for built-in hobs)
- Ensure proper ventilation

### Step 3: Gas Supply Installation
If you don't have gas supply:
- Install gas line from bottle/tank to kitchen
- Use correct pipe sizing (usually 8mm-15mm copper)
- Install gas isolation valve near stove
- Pressure test entire system

### Step 4: Stove Installation (1-2 hours)

**Built-In Gas Hob:**
- Position hob in countertop cutout
- Secure with mounting brackets
- Seal edges to prevent spills entering cabinet
- Connect gas supply line
- Test burner operation

**Freestanding Stove:**
- Position stove in designated space
- Level stove using adjustable feet
- Connect gas supply
- Connect electrical power (if applicable)
- Test all functions

**Connection Standards:**
- Flexible stainless steel connectors (where permitted)
- Rigid copper piping
- Approved gas fittings only
- Proper sealing and support

### Step 5: Testing & Commissioning
Comprehensive testing includes:
- Leak testing all connections with soap solution
- Gas pressure verification
- Ignition testing on all burners
- Flame pattern inspection
- Safety device testing (flame failure)
- Oven function testing (if applicable)

### Step 6: Customer Training
We teach you:
- How to light and adjust burners
- Safety features and operation
- Cleaning and maintenance tips
- Troubleshooting common issues
- Emergency gas shut-off location

### Step 7: COC Issuance
Certificate of Compliance issued documenting safe, compliant installation.

Thinking about switching from electric to gas? Here's what's involved:

**Good Candidates for Conversion:**

- Renovating your kitchen

## Gas Stove Safety & Maintenance (300 words)

### Safety Features in Modern Gas Stoves

**Flame Failure Device (FFD):**
Automatically cuts gas supply if flame extinguishes, preventing gas accumulation.

**Thermocouple Safety:**
Monitors flame presence and shuts off gas if flame is extinguished.

**Auto-Ignition:**
Electronic ignition eliminates need for matches or lighters.

**Pan Supports:**
Heavy cast iron supports provide stable cooking surface.

### Safe Gas Stove Operation

**DO:**
- Ensure good ventilation when cooking
- Keep burners clean for efficient combustion
- Check flame color (should be blue)
- Turn off gas when not in use
- Install isolation valve for easy shut-off

**DON'T:**
- Leave cooking unattended
- Use damaged or warped cookware
- Block ventilation
- Use stove for heating
- Ignore gas smells (act immediately)

### Maintenance Tips

**Weekly:**
- Wipe down surfaces after cooling
- Clean spillovers immediately
- Remove and wash pan supports

**Monthly:**
- Deep clean burner caps and heads
- Check burner flame quality
- Clean ignition electrodes

**Annually:**
- Professional service inspection
- Gas pressure check
- Connection inspection
- Safety device testing

## Frequently Asked Questions (300 words)

### Can I install a gas stove myself?
No. Gas stove installation must be performed by qualified, SAQCC Gas registered installers. DIY installation is illegal and dangerous.

### Do I need special pots for gas stoves?
No. Gas stoves work with all cookware types - stainless steel, cast iron, aluminum, copper, and even glass (though not recommended).

### How much gas does a stove use?
Average family cooking uses 4-6kg LPG per month, costing R100-R150 monthly.

### Can I use a gas stove during loadshedding?
Yes! Most gas stoves don't need electricity. Even models with electronic ignition can be lit with matches during power outages.

### What size gas bottle do I need?
Most homes use 9kg or 19kg LPG bottles. Larger homes or heavy cooking may prefer 48kg bottles or bulk tanks.

### Do gas stoves need ventilation?
Yes. Proper kitchen ventilation is essential for gas stoves to expel combustion products. Extractor fans or windows should be used.

### How long do gas stoves last?
With proper maintenance, quality gas stoves last 15-20 years or more.

### What if I smell gas?
1. Don't light flames or switches
2. Turn off gas supply immediately
3. Open windows for ventilation
4. Evacuate if strong smell
5. Call us for emergency inspection

### Can I connect my gas stove to piped gas?
Yes, if you have piped natural gas or LPG reticulation. We install for both bottled and piped gas.

### Do you remove old electric stoves?
Yes, we safely disconnect and remove old electric stoves as part of the installation service.
