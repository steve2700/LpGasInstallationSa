## Why Gas Appliances Need Regular Maintenance (300 words)

Gas appliances are sophisticated systems that require periodic professional attention:

### Safety Reasons:

**Gas Leaks:**
Connections can loosen over time, seals degrade, and pipes corrode. Regular inspections catch leaks early.

**Carbon Monoxide Risk:**
Incomplete combustion produces dangerous CO. Proper burner maintenance ensures clean, safe combustion.

**Flame Failure Devices:**
Safety devices must function correctly. Testing ensures they'll shut off gas if flames extinguish.

**Ventilation Issues:**
Blocked flues or insufficient ventilation cause dangerous gas accumulation. Inspections verify proper airflow.

### Performance Reasons:
- Remote control battery and function testing
- Blower motor cleaning and lubrication (if applicable)
- Oxygen depletion sensor testing
- Safety shut-off testing
- Flame pattern adjustment

## Common Gas Appliance Problems (300 words)

### Gas Geyser Issues:

**No Hot Water:**
- Pilot light extinguished
- Thermocouple failure
- Gas supply problem
- Water flow sensor issue
- Solution: Professional diagnosis and repair

**Insufficient Hot Water:**
- Undersized unit for demand
- Low gas pressure
- Limescale buildup reducing efficiency
- Burner issues
- Solution: Descaling service or system assessment

**Fluctuating Temperature:**
- Water pressure variations
- Flow sensor calibration needed
- Mixing valve problems
- Solution: Professional calibration

**Strange Noises:**
- Limescale buildup (popping/crackling)
- Water hammer in pipes
- Burner issues
- Solution: Descaling and inspection

### Gas Stove Issues:

**Burners Won't Light:**
- Ignition electrode dirty or misaligned
- Clogged burner ports
- Faulty igniter
- Gas supply problem
- Solution: Cleaning and adjustment

**Yellow/Orange Flames:**
- Clogged burner ports (should be blue)
- Incorrect air-to-gas ratio
- Dirty burners
- Solution: Professional cleaning and adjustment

**Uneven Heating:**
- Blocked burner ports
- Misaligned burner caps
- Gas pressure issues
- Solution: Cleaning and calibration

### Gas Fireplace Issues:

**Won't Ignite:**
- Pilot light problems
- Thermocouple failure
- Ignition system fault
- Gas supply interruption
- Solution: Professional diagnostics

**Glass Blackening Quickly:**
- Incomplete combustion
- Improper gas-to-air ratio
- Burner needs cleaning
- Ventilation problems
- Solution: Burner cleaning and adjustment

**Weak Flames:**
- Low gas pressure
- Clogged burner orifices
- Blocked air intake
- Solution: System inspection and cleaning

### Gas Braai Issues:

**Uneven Heating:**
- Clogged burner ports
- Spider webs in venturi tubes
- Gas pressure imbalance
- Solution: Thorough cleaning

**Ignition Problems:**
- Dirty ignition electrodes
- Blocked burner ports near igniter
- Battery replacement needed
- Solution: Cleaning and battery replacement

**Gas Smell:**
- Leak at connections
- Worn hoses
- Loose fittings
- Solution: **Immediate professional inspection**

## Safety Warning Signs (200 words)

### Immediate Action Required - Call Professional:

**Gas Smell:**
Most serious warning sign. If you smell gas:
1. Don't light flames or operate electrical switches
2. Turn off gas supply at bottle/meter
3. Open windows for ventilation
4. Evacuate if strong smell
5. Call us immediately

**Yellow/Orange Flames (Should Be Blue):**
Indicates incomplete combustion producing carbon monoxide.
- Don't use appliance
- Call for service immediately

**Soot Buildup:**
Black soot around appliance indicates combustion problems.
- Stop using appliance
- Schedule immediate inspection

**Pilot Light Won't Stay Lit:**
Safety device may be failing or thermocouple issue.
- Don't bypass safety features
- Call for professional repair

**Excessive Condensation:**
May indicate ventilation problems or incomplete combustion.
- Check ventilation
- Schedule inspection

**Unusual Sounds:**
Popping, whistling, or roaring sounds indicate problems.
- Monitor closely
- Schedule service soon

**Headaches/Nausea When Using:**
Possible carbon monoxide exposure.
- Stop using appliance immediately
- Ventilate area
- Seek medical attention if severe
- Call for emergency inspection

**Never Ignore Safety Warning Signs** - they indicate real dangers.
